August 23, 2025 - Linux

I’ve officially made the switch to Linux! Ever since I first watched that PewDiePie video about Linux, I’ve wanted to try it out for myself—and now I finally can.

Windows has always been a reliable companion, but one major drawback for me was how poorly it handled LaTeX. Most of the time, I had to rely on Overleaf for my projects, but being tied to an internet connection was frustrating. I did attempt setting up LaTeX on Windows using VS Code, but the process was a headache—it required WSL and a lot of unnecessary steps. Even after getting it working, my system eventually crashed, forcing me to reinstall Windows (thankfully, my digital license saved the day).

Now, I’m running Fedora 42, a Linux distro recommended by a friend, and the experience has been smooth and refreshing. No extra bloat, no complicated setups—just a clean, minimal, and reliable system. So far, I’m absolutely loving it.

“Windows holds your hand, but Linux lets you take the wheel.”
— Unknown

August 1, 2025 - Hard Work

Another important lesson in life: hard work doesn’t guarantee success. I remember being told by my teachers to work hard, and I’d achieve my goal. Even I told many of my friends to work hard to achieve their desired academic scores. But now I’m realizing that I was wrong — even if you put all your efforts into getting to your desired goal, you might not be able to achieve it. NOTHING IS GUARANTEED!!!

Hard work does pay off, but it doesn’t pay off by guaranteeing success. If you work hard in a field, you might not reach where you wanted to reach, but when you look back, you’ll have come a long way. Hard work pays off by making you a better person. You might not be the best, but by doing hard work, you’ll at least be better than who you were before starting off. And in that process, you might turn out to be lucky and reach your goal.

"Success is a journey, not a destination. The doing is often more important than the outcome."
— Arthur Ashe
